Steps to Using The Reduction Toolkit

Step One: Start by Logging In

To use the Reduction Toolkit you must first login using your username and password. If you are a Director of Materials Management (DMM) with BHS, you will be given a username and password that will enable you to act on behalf of your facility. If you are a DMM and do not know your login credentials, please contact jdprice@baptisthealthsystem.com

The Reduction Toolkit is made up of four separate "tools" or sections:

  1. Action Profile
  2. Target Non Intake Items
  3. Target Intake Items
  4. Review Targeted Items

We will now be taking an in-depth look at each one of these sections. Note that you can jump ahead to any section you are interested in by clicking the hyperlinks on the left side of the documentation.

Step Two: The Action Profile

After logging in, you will be redirected to the Action Profile page. You can think of this page as your HQ for making all decisions related to incoming or outgoing inventory. Below you'll see a screenshot of an example Action Profile. You will notice that the profile consists of a header at the top of the screen with several metrics in a list, and two tables below that. These features will be explained over the next several steps. On the left is the navigation drawer that you can use to navigate the website. Remember that you can always get back to the documentation by clicking the tab labeled "Documentation" if you are ever stuck or confused.

NOTE: Portions of screengrabs in the documentation have been redacted to protect sensitive company information.

Now that your're familiar with the layout of the website, it's time to focus on targeting items for reduction. The Reduction Toolkit (RT) takes your inventory counts submitted bi-annually and pulls the items located in your Surgery and Cath Lab (SLB and NCB are solely items located in surgery). These items are then sorted for highest Extended Cost (LUOM Price * Quantity) and then seperated into two buckets.

  • Top 100 Items by Ext Cost that did not receive intake in FY2020
  • Top 100 Items by Ext Cost that did receive intake in FY2020

Therefore you have two options when targeting items that you'd like to reduce. You can target "No Intake Items", or you can target "Intake Items" (AKA items that are seeing intake but we might consider being held in excess). Your first priority should be to target and reduce inventory that is not seeing intake, so let's do that in the next step.

Step Three: Target Items for Reduction

Still on the Action Profile page, you can navigate to the Target No Intake page by clicking the tab on the navigation sidedrawer labeled "Target No Intake". This will take you to a screen with a list of items being considered "Non-Intake" items. The list consists of 100 items that are flagged as no intake and are sorted by highest extended cost. In order for an item to be considered to have no intake it must meet the following requirements:

  • Zero purchase orders (POs) for FY2020 (source data from Novus Reach)
  • Zero items issued to your facility (issues) for FY2020 (I9000 report from IMMS)

Note that if an item is in multiple locations or "bins" in your facility, then the system will group them together if their unit of measures (UOM) are the same. This helps to handle items with the same IMMS number that are listed multiple times on your count sheet.

Below is what the Target No Intake page should look like:

Now that you've got your list of items with no intake, you can begin targeting them. Each DMM should go through their list and check to see if any of the items in the list can be reduced at their facility. For our example, let's say that you are checking the list and see that the item in the screenshot above labeled "RELOAD, STAPLE ARTICULATING..." can be reduced. You would then click the checkbox in the item's card labeled "Target Item". After checking the box, the box will have a blue checkmark in it and the system will update automatically. We don't have to click anything after checking the box, the system saves automatically and we can continue on in the process.

After going through the list and selecting all of the items that you'd like to reduce from your facility, you can then click on the tab labeled "Review Targets" in your navigation sidedrawer on the left of your page.

Step Four: Review Targets

After clicking on the "Review Targets" tab, you should now be looking at a page with the title "Review Target Items" at the top of screen. See below:

You'll see the item that you targeted previously, as well as any other items that you have targeted.

When you want to reduce an item, you will need to submit what is called a "movement plan". A movement plan will help you keep track of an item during the reduction process and also measure how much inventory you have reduced over a given period.

To submit a movement plan, click the blue button labeled "Set Movement Plan" on the bottom of the item's card.

You will now be taken to the page where you can submit your movement plan. You'll see three sections of information on this page:

  • Item Information
  • BHS Intake and Inventory History
  • Tenet Purchase Order History

The first section under the title "Set Movement Plan for Item:" is listing out the pertinent information for the item you have selected. Which will include Mfr Cat No, IMMS #, and unit of measure information.

The second section is under the title "BHS Facilities Showing Inventory History or Intake History". It's listing facilities within Baptist Health System that have shown the selected item in their most recent submitted inventory count. Note that even if the facility had zero units in their inventory, they will still show up if they've had the imms number in their count at some point, as we can see with facility 939 (BMC). Also worth noting, all measures mentioned above are shown in their lowest unit of measure. You can confirm this by referencing the UOM information given at the top of this page.

The third section is under the label "Tenet Facilities Showing Purchase Order History" (pictured below). This section will show you if any facilities within Tenet have a made purchase order for the selected item in FY2020. In our example, you can see that Abrazo Arrowhead Campus is showing the highest units ordered last year. It is worth noting that all markets in Tenet are represented except for the Detroit Market. We are still working on integrating their Lawson system with IMMS.

Also note that you can download the DMM contact list by clicking the gray outline button labeled "Download Tenet DMM Contact Info". The contact sheet is accurate as of 3/1/2021 and includes email/phone info for all DMM's in Tenet.

Finally, we arrive to the bottom of the page, the Movement Plan Form (pictured above). You should see a form with three fields:

  1. Quantity
  2. Decision
  3. System Destination Facility
Quantity

The quantity field is asking how many units of the selected item you are seeking to reduce from your quantity. Again, these units are in lowest unit of measure. You will be required to enter an intenger into this field that is higher than 0 and lower than your current inventory quantity which is listed in the item information at the top of the page. If you accidentally enter a quantity greater than your inventory level you will be shown an error page that will instruct you to try again or reference the documentation. Note that the Reduction Toolkit lets you set multiple Movement Plans on a single item. In order to prevent users from overdrafting their stocked inventory, the application will throw the aforementioned error page if a user makes multiple requests whose cumulative quantity is greater than facilities' inventory.

Decision

The decision is which method you would like to use to reduce the item's inventory. You have four options to choose from and they are listed in descending order of most optimal outcome to least optimal outcome:

Ship to a facility within the system

Choose this option if there is a BHS facility that is showing large intake of the selected item. This should be your first priority if possible.

Ship to a Tenet Facility

Choose this option if there is a Tenet facility that is showing large intake of the selected item. This should be your second priority if possible.

Sell to a Third Party Vendor

Choose this option if there is not enough intake in any BHS facilities or Tenet facilities. This should be your third choice.

Discard the Item

Choose this option if you have exhausted all other options. This should be your last resort.

System Destination Facility

After making a decision, the final field will be system destination facility. This field is potentially tricky and may lead to errors if not handled correctly. Its purpose is to help facilitate transfers within the Baptist Health System by declaring which facility you are attempting to transfer to. For example, our item is showing significant intake in BHS at Northeast Baptist. Knowing that, we would want to identify NBH as our destination facility by clicking the dropdown menu and selecting "Northeast Baptist Hospital".

But what if you are not shipping the item within BHS you might ask? Because this field cannot be left blank we need to indicate that we are not shipping within the system and are either shipping to a Tenet facility or selling/discarding the item. We can do this by clicking the dropdown and selecting "Shipping to a Tenet Facility" if we are sending to a Tenet facility or we can select "Selling or Discarding" if we are selling the item or discarding it.

Once you have filled out all the fields you can press the blue button labeled "Submit" to submit the form. For our example, let's say that you want to sell 5 EA of this item. To do so, you would enter 5 in the quantity field, select the radio button labeled "Sell to a third party vendor" and the select "Selling or Discarding" in the System Destination Facility dropdown. You can then click the submit button to submit the form.

Once you've submitted the form, you will taken back to the review targets page. You'll now be able to see that there is a "1" next to section of the card labeled "Plans Set".

Remember that you can set multiple movement plans on a single item. While the "Plans Set" counter will show you how many plans you have submitted for a single item, you can click on the gray "See All Plans" button on the bottom of the item card to get a detailed breakout of the plans.

In the next section, let's talk more about movement plans and what we can do with them after creating them.

Step Five: Movement Plans

Now that you've created your first movement plan, let's look more at how we can manage them in the Action Profile page. Click the tab labeled "Action Profile" in the navigation sidedrawer to be taken to your profile.

The first thing you'll notice is that two parts of the profile have changed:

First, you'll see that in the upper righthand side of the page, the metric labeled "You have targeted $_____ of inventory" has updated to reflect the new item that you just targeted. The metric is blue because it also serves as a link which you can click on to get a detailed breakout of the items that you have targeted.

Editing Movement Plans

Second, you'll notice that there is a new movement plan in your table at the bottom of the screen labeled "My Outgoing Requests". This is the movement plan you just submitted to reduce by selling 5 EA units. If you need to change the amount you'd like to reduce then you can do so by clicking the yellow button with the pencil icon on the righthand side of the page. If you need to change your decision to sell the item to discarding or transfering it instead, you will need to delete the item by clicking the red trashcan icon and submitting a new plan.

Finalizing Movement Plans

You'll notice that the status associated with this plan is currently "outstanding". Plans that are transfers to Tenet facilities, selling or discarding, will be considered "outstanding" until you finalize them by clicking the button labeled "Finalize". It is important to note that you should not finalize the movement plan until you have physically removed the item from your facility. In our example, this would look like reaching out to a third party vendor, receiving a quote, receiving approval from your CFO, and then physically shipping the item to the third party vendor. Once that has been accomplished you will click the Finalize button to indicate that you have completed the reduction of the item.

After finalizing the movement plan, you will notice several things happen:

First of all, you can see that you no longer have that item queued up in your "Outgoing Requests" table. Second, you will see that the item was removed from your targeted metric in the top righthand side of the page. The ext cost was removed and was added to the metric labeled "You have removed $____ of inventory". Notice that this metric also allows you to click on it and be shown a breakout of the items that are making up your completed movement plans. After clicking on the metric you will also notice that you can export the table of completed plans out to an Excel Spreadsheet. This should be helpful in notifiying accounting of the changes that you have made.

That's not the only place the application is updating after completing a movement plan. By clicking on the "Review Targets" tab in the navigation sidedrawer you will see that the field labeled "Remaining Quantity" in the item card has been reduced by 5 EA units.

Hiding a Targeted Item When You are Done With It

The entire application has updated to reflect your changes. You are free to set as many movement plans as your inventory will allow. When you are satisfied with the level of inventory that the item is now set at, you can hide an item from your "Review Targets" list by clicking the blue "X" in the top righthand side of the item's card. This will help you keep your targeted items list from getting cluttered with items that you are through targeting. It will also help you work through your "No Intake" and "High Ext" lists while keeping things nice and organized.

Conclusion

That brings the current tutorial to a close. That is essentially everything you would need to know for targeting an item, setting a movement plan, and finalizing it once you have completed the reduction. One last thing I would like to cover though is how transfers work within the Baptist Health System. We've covered reduction through transfers to Tenet facilities, selling, and discarding items, but now I'd like to focus on transferring an item from one facility within BHS to another. These movement plans work slightly different than movement plans that are directed towards Tenet facilities or selling/discarding. The reason they are different is they allow one DMM to respond to a movement plan set by another. Let's take a deeper look into how that might work.

Step Six: Transferring Items within BHS

Let's look at another example, but this time let's target an "Intake" item instead of a "No Intake" item. We are also going to transfer it within BHS instead of selling it to a third party vendor.

The process for targeting intake items is exactly the same as it is for targeting no intake items. You begin by clicking the tab in the navigation sidedrawer labeled "Target Intake". After arriving to the page, you can go down the list, one by one, or if you have a specific item or type of item you are looking for you can search for it in the filter box. There's a certain IMMS # I have in mind, so I am going to enter it in the field under the label "Imms contains" and then press the blue "Filter" button. See below:

You'll target the item by checking the box labeled "Target Item" at the bottom of the item card. After doing this, you will click the tab in the navigation sidedrawer labeled "Review Targets" to see the item you just targeted. Again, this is the same process as Targeting No Intake items.

Set the Movement Plan

Once on the "Review Targets" page, click the blue button labeled "Set Movement Plan" on the item you just targeted. You will then be taken to the set movement plan form.

You will then be taken the movement plan form where you will make your decision on how you want to reduce this item. For our example, let's pretend you are the DMM at facility 954. We will call you DMM A. You are looking for a facility to send this item to within the BHS system. By looking at the movement history below you can see that facility 939 showed the greatest intake for the BHS system last year. Knowing that, you should try and send the excess inventory to the DMM at facility 939, let's call them DMM B.

Maybe you want to send DMM B two boxes of this item (LUOM 24 EA). The movement plan form you would submit for that scenario would look like this:

Here you are sending a request to DMM B that you would like to send them 24 EA of the item. Click submit to send the form and be taken back to the review targets page.

You'll see that the movement plan has been recorded for the item, as the "Plans Set" counter now shows 1. Click the tab labeled "Action Profile" on the navigation sidedrawer to be taken back to your profile.

Once on your Action Profile page, you will see the new movement plan under your table labeled "My Outgoing Requests". See below:

Responding to BHS System Requests

We are close to removing this item, but are still waiting to finalize it. As mentioned before, finalizing the movement plan to facility 939 will work slightly different because it is a movement plan within the BHS system. If you were to try and click that movement plan's Finalize Button right now, it would not work because the movement request still has a status of "outstanding". You will not be able to finalize a BHS system movement plan until the DMM of that facility approves your request. If you were to look at the Action Profile of the DMM at facility 939, their table labeled "My Incoming Requests" would look like this:

You'll see that your request has populated their table and is waiting for their respone. They have two options they can use to respond, "Approve" or "Reject", and these are chosen by picking their respective buttons on the righthand side of the movement plan. If the DMM at facility 939 decides to accept the movement plan, they will need to enter the number of units into the field labeled "Accept Qty". Note that this figure will be in lowest unit of measure already for you.

For our example, let's say that the DMM at facility 939 decides that they can take on only 20 units instead of the full 24. They would then enter four in the "Accept Qty" field and would then press the green "Accept" button. You may be expecting the metric in the top righthand side of the page labeled "You have accepted $___ of inventory" to update and reflect the inventory that the DMM has just accepted, but that will not happen. This movement plan still needs to be finalized by the sending DMM for it to be recognized by the application. Let's do that next

Finalizing the Movement Plan

Back on the Action Profile for the sending DMM at facility 954, you can see that the DMM at facility 939 accepted the movement plan you sent them. You can see this by the "accepted" label in the "Status" column, See below:

You'll see that the DMM at facility 939 accepted 20 EA units. Awesome, the DMM has accepted your request and now you can physically ship them the accepted inventory. After you have physically shipped the inventory out of your facility to the destination, then you can click the Finalize Button to have the changes reflected throughout the application.

Conclusion

Congratulations, you just finalized your first movement plan! While the process might seem hard to remember at first, after completing several plans it should become very intuitive.

Like any software, there are bound to be bugs in the code. If you find any while using the application please report them at jdprice@baptisthealthsystem.com so we can get them fixed.
