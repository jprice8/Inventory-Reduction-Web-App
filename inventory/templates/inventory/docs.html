Steps to Using The Reduction Toolkit

Step One: Start by Logging In

To use the Reduction Toolkit you must first login using your username and password. If you are a Director of Materials Management (DMM) with BHS, you will be given a username and password that will enable you to act on behalf of your facility. If you are not a BHS DMM, you can still sign up, however you will not have access to the View, Move, and Act pages. As a non-BHS DMM you currently only have access to the "Outstanding Requests" page but we are working to expand those functions.

Step Two: Move to the View Page

After logging in, move to the View page by either clicking the "Get Started" button or by clicking the "View" tab on the nav bar at the top of your screen. Once you have been taken to the View page, you will see your facilities top 500 items from your last submitted inventory count sorted by extended cost (price * quantity). For facilities: BMC, NBH, RHH, and MTB, these items are located in either your Surgery Department or your Cath Lab. For facilities: NCB and SLB, these items are solely located in Surgery due to complications with your Cath Lab inventory counts. The View page will best be used as a reference for high extended cost items at your facility. You can see important information such as the items specific inventory count at the last submitted count (Noted by the Period column).

On the View page above, you can see the data table that is housing your inventory. The table will start off by showing the top 10 items sorted by extended cost. You can extend the amount of items the table is showing by adjusting the drop down button at the top titled "Show ___ entries". You can also sort the table by any of the columns. You can sort the columns by clicking on the column header and change the sort from ASCENDING to DESCENDING by clicking on the column header again. The data table also features a robust search function that allows you to instantly search your entire table for a desired entry. You can enter numbers or characters alike and the search will attempt to find a row that fulfills the search.

Step Three: Target Items for Reduction

Once you're familiar with the type of inventory that is being tracked, continue to the next tab on the Navigation Bar titled "Target". The Target page will list the top 100 items that are showing zero purchase orders and zero issues during FY 2020. A good way to think about The Reduction Toolkit is to picture a giant filter.

The diagram below depicts the filtering process that is used by The Reduction Toolkit. We are currently at the point of targeting the top 100 items with no intake.

You, as a DMM, should go through the list of no-intake items and check to see if the item is either:

  • Not moving
  • Excessively held and available for reduction

If the item qualifies for either, then you should check the box at the bottom of the individual item labeled "Target Item". This will flag the item for you and add it to your list of targeted items.

For our example let's say that you decide to target the item at the top of our list below, labeled "RELOAD, STAPLE ARTICULATING REINFORCED TRI 60MM"

Now that we have targeted our item for potential reduction, it's time to set a plan in place to actually execute the reduction. The first step in this process is to click the button near the top of the screen, but below the Navigation Bar, titled "Review Target Items".

Step Four: Set Movement Plans

Arguably the most important step, this is where the actual value for this application is derived. We should now be looking at a screen that has a giant label at the top titled "Review Target Items". We also should be able to see our item, the reload stapler, that we just targeted in the previous screen. See below:

For each item that we have targeted, there will initially be a red button at the bottom of the item labeled "Set Movement Plan". Click this button to set a movement plan.
