Steps to Using The Reduction Toolkit

Step One: Start by Logging In

To use the Reduction Toolkit you must first login using your username and password. If you are a Director of Materials Management (DMM) with BHS, you will be given a username and password that will enable you to act on behalf of your facility. If you are not a BHS DMM, you can still sign up, however you will not have access to the View, Move, and Act pages. As a non-BHS DMM you currently only have access to the "Outstanding Requests" page but we are working to expand those functions.

The Reduction Toolkit is split into four different sections:

  1. Action Profile
  2. Target Non Intake Items
  3. Target High Ext Items
  4. Review Targeted Items

We will now be taking an in-depth look at each one of these sections. Note that you can jump ahead to any section you are interested in by clicking the hyperlinks on the left side of the documentation.

Step Two: The Action Profile

After logging in, you will be redirected to the Action Profile page. You can think of this page as your HQ for making all decisions related to incoming or outgoing inventory. Below you'll see a screenshot of an example Action Profile. You will notice that the profile consists of a header at the top of the screen with several metrics in a list, and two tables below that. These features will be explained over the next several steps. On the left is the navigation drawer that you can use to navigate the website. Remember that you can always get back to the documentation by clicking the tab labeled "Documentation" if you are ever stuck or confused.

NOTE: Portions of screengrabs in the documentation have been redacted to protect sensitive company information.

Now that your're familiar with the layout of the website, it's time to focus on targeting items for reduction. The Reduction Toolkit (RT) takes your inventory counts submitted bi-annually and pulls the items located in your Surgery and Cath Lab (SLB and NCB are solely items located in surgery). These items are then sorted for highest Extended Cost (LUOM Price * Quantity) and then seperated into two buckets.

  • Items that did not receive intake in FY2020
  • Items that did receive intake in FY2020

Therefore you have two options when targeting items that you'd like to reduce. You can target "No Intake Items" or you can target "High Ext Items" (AKA items that are seeing intake but we might consider being held in excess). Your first priority should be to target and reduce inventory that is not seeing intake, so let's do that in the next step.

Step Three: Target Items for Reduction

Still on the Action Profile page, you can navigate to the Target No Intake page by clicking the tab on the navigation sidedrawer labeled "Target No Intake". This will take you to a screen with a list of items being considered "Non-Intake" items. The list consists of 100 items that are flagged as no intake and are sorted by highest extended cost. In order for an item to be considered to have no intake it must meet the following requirements:

  • Zero purchase orders (POs) for FY2020 (source data from Novus Reach)
  • Zero items issued to your facility (issues) for FY2020 (I9000 report from IMMS)

Please note that if one of these items is in multiple locations or "bins" in your facility, then the system will group them together if their unit of measures (UOM) are the same. This helps to handle items with the same IMMS number that are listed multiple times on your count sheet.

Now that you've got your list of items with no intake, you can begin targeting them. Each DMM should go through their list and check to see if any of the items in the list can be reduced at their facility. For our example, let's say that you are checking the list and see that the item in the screenshot above labeled "SLING, URETHRAL DESARA BLUE..." can be reduced. You would then click the checkbox in the item's card labeled "Target Item". After checking the box, the box will have a blue checkmark in it and the system will update automatically. We don't have to click anything after checking the box, the system saves automatically and we can continue on in the process.

After going through the list and selecting all of the items that you'd would like to reduce from your facility, you can then click on the tab labeled "Review Targets" in your navigation sidedrawer on the left of your page.

After clicking on the "Review Targets" tab, you should now be looking at a page with the title "Review Target Items" at the top of screen. See below:

You'll see the item that you targeted previously, as well as any other items that you have targeted.

When you want to reduce an item, you will need to submit what is called a "movement plan". A movement plan will help you keep track of an item during the reduction process and also measure how much inventory you have reduced over a given period.

To submit a movement plan, click the blue button labeled "Set Movement Plan" on the bottom of the item's card.

You will now be taken the page where you can submit your movement plan. You'll see three sections of information on this page:

  • Item Information
  • BHS Intake and Inventory History
  • Tenet Purchase Order History

The first section under the title "Set Movement Plan for Item:" is listing out the pertinent information for the item you have selected. Which will include Mfr Cat No, IMMS #, and unit of measure information.

The second section under the title "BHS Facilities Showing Inventory History or Intake History" is pretty much self explanatory. It is listing facilities within Baptist Health System that have shown the selected item in their most recent submitted inventory count. Note that even if the facility had zero units in their inventory, they will still show up if they've had the imms number in their count at some point, as we can see with facility 939 (BMC). Also worth noting, all measures mentioned above are shown in their lowest unit of measure. You can confirm this by referencing the UOM info at the top of the screen.

The third section is under the label "Tenet Facilities Showing Purchase Order History". This section will show you if any facilities within Tenet have a made purchase order for the selected item in FY2020. In our example, you can see that the system is not able to find any matches for Tenet facilities and is informing us of that. It is worth noting that all markets in Tenet are represented except for the Detroit Market. We are still working on integrating their Lawson system with IMMS.

Finally, we arrive to the bottom of the page, the Movement Plan. You should see a form with three fields:

  1. Quantity
  2. Decision
  3. System Destination Facility
Quantity

The quantity field is asking how many units of the selected item you are seeking to reduce from your quantity. Again, these units are in lowest unit of measure. You will be required to enter an intenger into this field that is higher than 0 and lower than your current inventory quantity which is listed in the item information at the top of the page. If you accidentally enter a quantity greater than your inventory level you will be shown an error page that will instruct you to try again or reference the documentation. Note that the Reduction Toolkit lets you set multiple Movement Plans on a single item. In order to prevent users from overdrafting their stocked inventory, the application will throw the aforementioned error page if a user makes multiple requests whose cumulative quantity is greater than facilities' inventory.

Decision

The decision is which method you would like to use to reduce the item's inventory. You have four options to choose from and they are listed in descending order of most optimal outcome to least optimal outcome:

  • Ship to a facility within the system

Choose this option if there is a BHS facility that is showing large intake of the selected item. This should be your first priority if possible.

  • Ship to a Tenet Facility

Choose this option if there is a Tenet facility that is showing large intake of the selected item. This should be your second priority if possible.

  • Sell to a third party vendor

Choose this option if there is not enough intake in any BHS facilities or Tenet facilities. This should be your third choice.

  • Discard the item

Choose this option if you have exhausted all other options. This should be your last resort.

System Destination Facility

After making a decision, the final field will be system destination facility. This field is potentially tricky and may lead to errors if not handled correctly. Its purpose is to help facilitate transfers within the Baptist Health System by declaring which facility you are attempting to transfer to. For example, our item is showing no intake in BHS. However, let's say NCB was showing a large amount of issued units in 2020, then we would click the drop down and select North Central Baptist Hospital. But what if you are not shipping the item within BHS you might ask? Because this field cannot be left blank we need to indicate that we are not shipping within the system and are either shipping to a Tenet facility or selling/discarding the item. We can do this by clicking the dropdown and selecting "Shipping to a Tenet Facility" if we are sending to a Tenet facility or we can select "Selling or Discarding" if we are selling the item or discarding it.

Once you have filled out all the fields you can press the blue button labeled "Submit" to submit the form. For our example, I will indicate that I want to sell 5 EA of this item. Therefore, I will enter 5 in quantity, select the button labeled "Sell to a third party vendor" and then select "Selling or Discarding" in the System Destination Facility dropdown. I'll then click Submit to submit the form.
